# The Overlap Voice - 81 Questions

## Router Question (asked first)
1. Imagine this process did its job perfectly. How does your life look different or better in the next three to six months?

## Step 1: Who You Are
2. How long have you been doing this work? What was the path that brought you to doing what you do?
3. If you couldn't use your job title, how would you describe what you actually do for people?
4. When someone works with you, what changes for them? What becomes possible that wasn't before?
5. What specific compliments from your best clients make you feel the most proud of your work? Share the actual testimonials or feedback if you can.
6. Who is your work NOT for? What kind of client would you turn away?
7. How do you describe what you do to a stranger at a barbecue?
8. What credentials, certifications, or formal training do you have?

## Step 2: What You Do
9. When someone hires you, what do they actually get? What's the thing you deliver?
10. Walk me through your process from the moment someone hires you to the moment the work is done.
11. What's included in your core offering? What's NOT included?
12. How long does it typically take from start to finish?
13. What do you need from the client for the work to go well?
14. Do you offer different tiers or packages? If so, what distinguishes them?

## Step 3: Market Position
15. If someone can't work with you, what are their other options? Who else does what you do?
16. In your opinion, what do those alternatives do well?
17. In your opinion, what do those alternatives do poorly or miss entirely?
18. What do you do differently than those alternatives?
19. Why does that difference matter? What does it make possible?
20. How do you describe your category when people ask what industry you're in?

## Step 4: Pricing & Value
21. How do you charge? (Hourly, project, retainer, etc.)
22. What's the typical range for a project or engagement?
23. What factors change the price? (Scope, timeline, complexity, etc.)
24. What's the smallest project you'll take on? Is there a minimum?
25. How do clients typically measure ROI or success when working with you?
26. What are your payment terms? (50% upfront, net 30, etc.)
27. Do you charge for travel, expenses, or other add-ons?
28. Do you offer discounts for nonprofits, bulk projects, or referrals?
29. When was the last time you raised your rates? Why?

## Step 5: Voice & Communication
30. Share 3 examples of writing that sounds like you — emails, blog posts, social content, or proposals.
31. Share an example of writing that doesn't sound like you at all.
32. What words or phrases do you use all the time in your work?
33. What words or phrases do you hate? What language feels wrong for your work?
34. How would you describe your tone when you're communicating with clients?
35. Are there metaphors or analogies you use to explain your work?
36. Do you use humor in your client communication? If so, what kind?

## Step 6: Proof & Stories
37. Tell me about 2-3 of your best client stories — or even just one if you're early in your work.
38. What measurable results have you helped clients achieve?
39. What's the best testimonial you've ever received?
40. What industries or client types have you worked with most?
41. Do you have a portfolio, case studies, or work samples you share with prospects?
42. Have you been featured in any publications, podcasts, or media?
43. Have you won any awards or recognition in your field?
44. What's the profile of your ideal client? (Size, industry, stage, etc.)

## Step 7: Client Experience
45. What happens in your first conversation with a potential client?
46. How do you know if someone is a good fit to work with you?
47. What are the red flags that tell you someone is NOT a good fit?
48. Once someone hires you, what's the onboarding process like?
49. How often do you communicate with clients during a project?
50. What tools or platforms do you use to manage client work?
51. What happens when a project wraps up? How do you hand things off?
52. Do clients typically come back for more work? What brings them back?

## Step 8: Meta Questions
53. What have clients or colleagues told you that you do differently than others in your field?
54. What do people most often misunderstand about your work?
55. What part of your work gives you the most energy? What drains you?
56. What have you learned about your work in the last year that changed how you do it?
57. If this goes perfectly, where does your work go in the next 2-3 years?

## Step 9: Documentation & Digital Presence
58. Where do you currently store information you need to reference repeatedly — pricing, processes, templates, client details?
59. What templates, scripts, or documents do you reuse across clients?
60. What questions do you answer over and over again?
61. What objections do you hear most often from potential clients, and how do you handle each one?
62. What's your website URL?
63. What's your LinkedIn profile URL?
64. What's your Instagram handle or profile URL?
65. What's your Facebook page or profile URL?
66. What's your YouTube channel URL?
67. What's your TikTok handle or profile URL?
68. What's your Google Business Profile URL?
69. List any review sites where you have profiles (Yelp, Google Reviews, industry-specific platforms).
70. List any press coverage, interviews, podcasts, or articles featuring you or your work.
71. Any other relevant online presence? Portfolio sites, directories, Substack, Medium, etc.
72. List books that have shaped how you think about your work.
73. List specific podcast episodes you reference frequently or that influenced your approach.
74. List YouTube videos that influenced your approach or that you share with clients.
75. List blog posts or articles you reference frequently in your work.
76. List thought leaders, authors, or creators you follow closely and whose work influences yours.

## Step 10: Operations & Logistics
77. How many clients can you serve at once?
78. Do you have a waitlist? How far out are you typically booked?
79. Do you work solo or do you have a team? Who does what?
80. Do you partner with or refer work to other professionals?
81. Where are you based? Do you work remotely, locally, or both?
82. What's your typical availability for new projects?
